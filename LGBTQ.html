LGBTQ Terms and Definitions

Sexualities

NOTE: all terms with sexual are atractions in a sexual sense. Replacing the sexual with romantic changes it to a romantic sense.

Lesbian
Types of Speech: Noun, Adjective
Lesbian is a term that comes from Lesbos, the home of Sappho, a historical lesbian. A lesbian is a female who is atracted to other females, in a sexual sense.
Lesbish
Type of Speech: Adjective
First coined in Jocelyn Samara's web comic, Rain, Lesbish is derived from the word lesbian. a lesbish person is a female who is atracted to females in a romantic sense.
Gay
Type of Speech: Adjective
Gay, derived from a word from happy, is used to describe someone who is atracted to their own gender in a sexual sense.
Homosexual
Type of Speech: Adjective
From a prefix meaning same, has the same meaning as gay.
Bisexual
Type of Speech: Adjective
From a prefix meaning 2, describes someone atracted to 2 or more genders.
Pansexual
Type of Speech: Adjective
From a prefix meaning all, someone who is atracted to people regardless of gender.
Asexual
Type of Speech: Adjective
From a prefix meaning none, someone who is atracted to no one sexually.
